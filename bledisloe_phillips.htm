Campaign to Protect Rural England - Gloucestershire

Bledisloe Cup - 2004 Report by Prof. Adrian Phillips CBE

Acknowledgements

My thanks to Margaret Boakes for her efficient organisation of the Bledisloe Cup for 2004. She has done an admirable job, taking over smoothly from Graham Stockham who had indeed set a very high standard. And thanks, too, to the many judges who assessed the entries at an earlier stage. Their detailed and conscientious analyses were very helpful in preparation for my inspections and in the field.

General remarks

As always, judging the Bledisloe Cup was fascinating and difficult. Maybe this year it was more challenging than usual because of the very different kinds of villages that made it to the final: three from the Cotswolds, one from the Vale of Gloucester and two from the Forest of Dean area.

Villages have been judged against the agreed criteria: these can be summed up in the answer to the question: 'what community has done the most to bring added value to the village environment?' and not - of course - 'what village looks prettiest?' I have tried to take into account that some villages have a much tougher challenge than others, but at the same time, one can only judge by results.

As it turns out, all three winners this year are Cotswold villages, but all the others were impressive runners up. It is really important that they keep trying — who knows, with a little more effort next year all three Bledisloe winners may come from the Vale and Forest of Dean parts of Gloucestershire.

Final Results

I recommend that the following villages should be the winners of the Bledisloe Cup for 2004:

  • Large Village: South Cerney
  • Medium-sized Village: Somerford Keynes
  • Small Village: Cold Aston

Adrian Phillips CBE
