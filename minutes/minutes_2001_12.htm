Dr S. Pickering from the Cotswold Water Park Society gave a talk to assembled Parish Councillors on the types of funding available for nature conservation and provision of green spaces. There were various grants ranging from £3,000 to £25,000 and one up to £100,000 which could help communities provide a biodiversity habitat for wildlife sustainability. Dr Pickering would be willing to give guidance to the Parish Council on how to apply for available grants. Cllr Harris asked if the old silt beds to the rear of the CAMAS site would be a suitable area for funding. Dr Pickering considered that it was suitable and an ideal scheme for match funding. Cllr Nicholas asked if the Cotswold Water Park Society would be prepared to manage the site on behalf of the Parish Council. Dr Pickering replied that the CWP would be interested and the management could be funded from any commuted fund which might be gained from the developers. Dr Pickering confirmed that he could carry out a water vole survey of the Upper Ups and Downs which the Environment Agency had asked for. Cllr Mrs Gardiner suggested that the site be renamed Jubilee Park and this was received enthusiastically by some Parish Councillors although no decision had yet been taken on a title.

One member of the public attended the meeting.

The Chairperson Cllr Mrs Gardiner opened the meeting at 7.30pm.
