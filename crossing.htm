A PEDESTRIAN CROSSING IN SOUTH CERNEY?

Traffic surveys have been undertaken by Gloucestershire Highways in response to the proposal for a pedestrian crossing in the High Street. The results of the surveys are to be discussed at the next Parish Council meeting, on Wednesday 14th March. A representative of Gloucestershire County Council will attend that meeting. The following is a summary of Gloucestershire Highways’ initial report to the Parish Council:-

In October 2006 pedestrian crossing counts were carried out along High Street and Broadway Lane, and vehicle speeds and volumes assessed at the War Memorial and The Old Cross junctions. The pedestrian counts illustrated a low number of people crossing during the 12 hour period 07:00 – 19:00. Those that were crossing did so sporadically, and not at one particular point. Gloucestershire Highways believes that these findings present a problem in justifying a crossing in the High Street.

Over the years Gloucestershire County Council have noted that pedestrians will not go out of their way to use a crossing. There are examples of this across the County, for instance at Churchdown, where a puffin crossing was provided for an average of 400 users, and yet it is still seen by many as under-used. The South Cerney survey indicated just 150 users.

Furthermore, traffic volume and speeds along High Street are such that Gloucestershire Highways believes there is little justification for traffic calming, or evidence to suggest that traffic conditions make it difficult for people to cross the road. Its Road Safety and Accident Investigation officers have undertaken a site visit, to go through the surveys and view the pedestrian movement and traffic flows. Ann Edwards School has recently recruited a School Crossing Patrol to operate in the mornings, on the High Street, and this will address the issue to a significant extent.

Gloucestershire Highways has concluded that there is no data to evidence a significant safety problem in the High Street. There are many stretches of road across the County that have a worse accident record, and they believe that those should take priority.

Another matter is the question of possible traffic calming in Silver Street, by the Post Office. Pedestrians, particularly the elderly, going to the Post Office and the Church find it difficult to cross due to the humped-back bridge to the north which obscures their view of oncoming vehicles. The possibility of a built-out curb outside the Post Office, to slow traffic speeds and to assist those crossing, is being considered. Gloucestershire Highways believes that such a scheme has some merit, and could potentially be prioritised for GCC funding. It is something that it will be happy to discuss further at the meeting on 14th March.
