04/10/05 Rose The trucks, upon leaving the gravel pits, should be sprayed with water to remove dust/mud from them. They have a certain amount of responsibility to keep the roads clean. However as the region is agriculturally active - in autumn a lot of mud comes from tractors out planting/ploughing etc. The heavy traffic from locals and tourists also creates problems, as does muddy water run-off from fields. decaying leaves also provide a very slippery material. It's often difficult for the source of 'mud' to be tracked down, and therefore the liability. Speak to the CDC, they'll inform you about liability, and the companies responsibilities - but generally they do what they're meant to.
