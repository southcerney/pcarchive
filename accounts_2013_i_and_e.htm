Notes:
 
1.  

Interest paid on cash investments reflects the reduction in the bank rate. As funds held by the Parish Council do not qualify under the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) it was decided to reduce its risk to a bank failure by distributing its deposit funds to Barclays Bank, HSBC, Lloyds/TSB and Santander Bank.
2.   Cotswold District Council granted £6,500 to subsidise the rethatching of the front of the Village Hall roof and the Parish Council was successful in its application to Gloucestershire Environmental Trust Co. which also granted £12,000 for the work. Gloucestershire County Council awarded £1,350 as a contribution to emergency work carried out by the Parish Council to alleviate a serious flood threat. South Cerney Village Hall Committee repaid a grant of £1,832 it was awarded for the replacement of its failed boiler.
3.   Residents or relatives of residents pay for memorial trees to be planted.
4.   South Cerney Cricket Club and South Cerney Football Club pay for their portion of the premium.
5.   Under a plan to reduce green waste collection the Parish Council operates a composting scheme the result of which Gloucestershire County Council rewards the production by a weight measure calculation of its output.
6.   Refurbishment of Upper Up car park; emergency flood relief work over Christmas 2012 period.
7.   Grants as listed under Note 2 above offset most of the cost of the refurbishment.
8.   The Section 137 payments are well within the allowed limits for such disbursements. South Cerney United Church was awarded £800 to subsidise its boiler replacement.
9.   Further to the Parish Councils commitment to its youth and due to the withdrawal of the Gloucestershire County Councils youth provision, under the auspices of the Village Plan the Parish Council continued its support of activities and the youth workers.
10.   The Parish Council, through its Village Plan, continues to support groups. This grant was made to the South Cerney Play Group.
