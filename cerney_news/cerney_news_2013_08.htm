ANTISOCIAL PARKING

Those who park on pavements are causing a nuisance to pedestrians, especially those in wheelchairs or with prams. All such incidents should be reported by telephoning 101 quoting the vehicle registration, location and time of offence. Likewise if anyone is seen illegally interfering with property of any kind or suspected of such action, the person/s should be reported to the police emergency 999 giving as much relevant detail as possible.

PARISH PRECEPT

A public meeting is being planned for November to explain how the Parish Council makes its financial plans for the coming fiscal year starting April 2014. It will be shown how a grant from the Parish Council could help the aspirations of local groups which have the best interests of the Parish of South Cerney with Cerney Wick in mind. Parish Councillors will gauge the feelings of villagers if there should be a need to increase the amount collected in Council Tax which is currently £45.18 a year for a Band D dwelling. If a club or recognised grouping is intent on making a bid for a grant it is vitally important that a representative is present at the meeting. Be advised that failure to attend the meeting may exclude any bid for a grant..

SOUTH CERNEY COMMUNITY COMPOSTING SCHEME

This ever growing project is open to everyone in the village to bring their green waste which will then be converted into useful compost. Financed by the Parish Council and sponsored by Gloucestershire County Council (GCC) this scheme has the potential to earn money which can be used for other projects in our community.
There is no charge for bringing your green waste and the majority of the work involved in making the compost comes from local volunteers and some professional help. Anyone is welcome to come along and help, either as a one off or on a regular basis. For more information as to how you can help, call Mike Stuart on 860439.
When the material is fully composted we can claim money from GCC. We then have the compost to dispose of. Those who have helped in the making of the compost will get first call on having it for their gardens. Thereafter we will be able to offer any remaining compost to others at a realistic price which will be notified when the next batch is ready towards the end of the summer.
There is a limit as to what we can use in the composting procedure. Please do respect our request and bring only stuff we can use as listed below. It will also be appreciated if you can read the instructions at the Upper Up site and then leave your green waste in the reception spaces as indicated.

PARISH COUNCIL AUDIT

The auditor, Grant Thornton UK LLP, reported after its annual review of the accounts of the Parish Council that on the basis of its check, the opinion was that the information in the annual return was in accordance with proper practices. There were no matters which gave cause for concern and that relevant legislation and regulatory requirements had been met.
