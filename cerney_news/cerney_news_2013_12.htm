Cerney News Announcement
DECEMBER 2013

FLOODING UPDATE

Following the severe problems with backed up drains and overflowing waterways during the flood emergency last winter the Parish Council has been working with many agencies to minimise future reoccurrences. Geoffrey Clifton-Brown MP has been leading attempts to get Thames Water and the Environment Agency to carry out remedial works to pipes and water courses. He has held two Flooding Resilience meetings in the village hall and at the most recent demanded that Thames Water take more steps than have been carried out so far. The Environment Agency has approved the continued use of the spillway dug across the old rail track by Parish Council and piped by Glos Highways and have agreed to remove some gravel from the river. Thames Water has installed electronic flow meters in some main sewers to monitor potential emergencies as well as sealing lids on manholes in areas likely to flood.

A further public meeting will be held in April 2014 to ensure that improvement works continue. Notes of the two previous meetings can be read on the Parish Council web site www.southcerney.org.uk

R WAY UPDATE

The R Way youth group has two new assistant leaders as Jasmin Tresadern and Chris Wearing both passed their professional qualifications after attending a six month long training course run by Young Gloucestershire. A grant from Cotswold District Council financed the training and the transport. Chris and Jasmin are long term members of the R Way group and Chris is also a member of the Parish Council.

EMERGENCY PLAN

To ensure that the community is able to deal with any future crisis, an Emergency Plan is being prepared by a team of local people assisted by Gloucestershire Community Council. Details will be circulated at a later date but in the meantime it is strongly recommended that every one living in those parts of our village where flooding may occur should take immediate steps to purchase their own sandbags and consider having flood barriers fitted to all external doors. The likelihood of there being supplies of free sandbags in future emergencies is remote so please take action now.

THE UPPER UP PLAYING FIELD

The Upper Up playing field, totally owned by the Parish Council was judged as being runner up in the annual Gloucestershire Playing Fields Association’s Playing Field of the Year contest and a prize of £50 was awarded to the Parish Council. Work has started on the replacement of the equipment at the play ground owned by the people of Cerney Wick following some excellent fund raising plus grants from the Parish Council and from other sources.

HISTORICAL DOCUMENTS

A search of the hidden corners of Village Hall storage unearthed a very interesting collection of village papers. A full set of minute books recording every Parish Council meeting from its start in 1897 to when records went onto the computer were discovered. These will be taken to Gloucestershire Records at the end of January for safe keeping. Until then should anyone wish to inspect them please contact Mike Stuart on 01285 860439.

We have also found all the minute books of the Village Hall Committee going back to 1922 when the barn, as it was then, was given to the village for conversion into the Hall. Some of the problems and situations recorded in these records make interesting reading.
