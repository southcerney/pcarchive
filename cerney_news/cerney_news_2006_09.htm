Cerney News Announcement
September 2006


DOG MESS
Some irresponsible dog owners exercising their animals on the Upper Up playing fields are not collecting their pets’ excrement. It is totally abhorrent when pets’ faeces are left on the football fields for unsuspecting players to tread in. If this conduct persists then the Parish Council will take measures to restrict access to the playing fields for all pets. You have been warned.

RUBBISH COLLECTION
All residents are requested to only put out rubbish for removal the night before it is due for collection. When rubbish is left out for days animals are very likely to rip the plastic bags and scatter the contents making an unsightly mess. Some residents of Wildmoorway Lane and Edwards College have been noted for leaving their rubbish out too early and all are asked to stop this behaviour.

MUD ON THE SPINE ROAD
Mud on the Spine Road and other roads off is a problem which has exercised the Parish Council for some time. The Cotswold District Council’s Planning Enforcement Officer, Mr. Colin Davis wishes to be informed when such instances occur. He can be contacted by either emailing enforcement@cotswold.gov.uk or telephoning Cirencester 623000.

CRIME REPORTING
When any resident has been affected by or witnessed any sort of crime it is important that the police are informed. Besides the emergency numbers low level instances of crime or other antisocial behaviour should be reported to 0845 090 1234.

STREET LIGHTING
To report faults in street lighting please telephone Freephone 0800 252382 at any time. Each street light has an identity number and must be quoted when reporting any fault.
