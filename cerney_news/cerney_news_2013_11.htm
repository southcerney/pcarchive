Cerney News Announcement
NOVEMBER 2013

VILLAGE PLAN

At its meeting in October the Parish Council voted unanimously to adopt the new Parish Plan. Helen Richards (GRCC), who advised the Parish Council on its content will prepare a large display version which can be viewed in the Village Hall in the near future.

LOCAL GOVERNMENT BOUNDARY COMMISSION

The Parish Council responded to the consultation on new ward boundaries stating that the arbitrary numerical equation to divide the district into manageable wards was flawed in relation to South Cerney. On projected calculations South Cerney would be too large for one ward and part would have to be syphoned off, or another part of the district would be cobbled to it, in order that two representatives could be allocated. The changes envisaged did not enhance the democratic representation and was an unnecessary expense in these austere times. The treatment proposed was demeaning and should be resisted.

VILLAGE FLOODING

The Parish Council is continuing its discussions with statutory bodies including Thames Water, Gloucestershire County Council, Environment Agency and Cotswold District Council on how to alleviate or even remedy the constant threat of flooding in times of heavy rainfall. The Parish Council, with the help of local individuals, constantly badgers the authorities for answers to potential water flow problems.

PRECEPT MEETING

Invitations have been sent out to all clubs and societies who may have a cause to request a grant from the Precept. Applicants intending to make a bid must attend the meeting to qualify. Please remember that the public meeting will take place on Wednesday 13th November at 8 pm in the Village Hall.

R-WAY REPORT

R-Way’s membership is flourishing under the guidance of Mrs Emily Hallett, Jai Cunningham, Jasmin Tresadern and Chris Wearing. Eleven young people will be attending a residential course at Skern Lodge. The average attendance at local events was 10–15 from the senior group and at least 15 juniors. There are Halloween and Christmas parties planned. Road safety issues for the older group are regularly discussed. The juniors take part in bushcraft sessions at Cotswold Forest School under the guidance of Johno Kimberley.

NEW FACILITY AT THE MALLARDS

Plans continue to evolve and members of the sub-committee meet on a regular basis with the design architects. The foot print of the building has now been decided and the planning of the interior layout can proceed.

SUCCESS FOR CERNEY WICK'S APPLICATION FOR GRANTS TO EQUIP ITS PLAYING FIELD

Jenny Pegler has been spectacularly successful in acquiring grants for the re-equipping of the play area in Cerney Wick. The Parish Council congratulated Jenny on her efforts which will be fully explained at the Precept Meeting on Wednesday 13th November. Jenny is a wonderful example of how the Parish Council and viable schemes can cooperate to secure elusive funding for worthwhile projects.
