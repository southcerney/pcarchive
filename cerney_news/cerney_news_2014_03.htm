Cerney News Announcement
MARCH 2014

FLOODING

Further immediate action to relieve potential flooding to Box Bush, Robert Franklin Way and The Lennards was decided on by the Parish Council at its meeting on 12th February. The flood relief works carried out in 2012/13 had kept the river Churn below danger level but with the continuous rainfall and close monitoring of the Environment Agency information it was clear that another slipway had to be created if disaster was to be averted. All the relevant agreements from the agencies concerned were obtained and James Harris with a group of volunteers completed the task on Thursday 13th February. Thames Water has prepared a questionnaire for those of you whose properties have been adversely affected by the conditions. It is very important that these forms are completed and returned as they will be used to determine which parts of the village need to be protected in future.

WAR MEMORIAL

Work will start shortly on dismantling of the monument and removal of the surrounding posts and chains of the War Memorial at the junction of Broadway Lane and High Street. The health and safety paperwork issues have been approved and the company hope to start work on Monday 24th February. Centreline Architectural Sculpture will refurbish the memorial and store it until the highway alterations which are scheduled to begin in early March have been completed at the junction. It is anticipated that the work on the carriageway will take approximately four weeks to complete.

WORLD WAR I COMMEMORATIONS

The Parish Council appeals for the loan of any memorabilia or photographs relating to the 1914–1918 World War. If you are willing to lend them to the Parish Council for display please contact Cllr Peter Jay on 860641.

A417 MISSING LINK

The Parish Council unanimously supports Gloucestershire County Council in its campaign to secure funding to sort out the A417 “missing link”. The link is a 5km stretch of single carriageway on the A417 with the remaining route being a dual carriageway between the M4 at Swindon and M5 at Gloucester. The scheme, if it is added to the government’s list, will stand a much greater chance of securing funding when cash is allocated in spring 2015. The new road will be made up of a short bypass of Nettleton Bottom, widening of Birdlip Bypass, a new junction at Birdlip, a new junction replacing the Air Balloon roundabout and construction of an additional two lane carriageway down Crickley Hill. The result will be an uninterrupted dual carriageway all the way from the M5 at Gloucester to the M4 at Swindon. The Parish Council encourages everyone to show your support by signing up and pledge your backing on www.a417missinglink.co.uk

UPPER UP COMPOST SITE

Reluctantly the Parish Council agreed that it must close its composting site to any new deposits at Upper Up until the present wet conditions relent. It has become impossible to manage the material. As soon as conditions improve the site will be opened up again to receive material for composting. Thank you for your understanding in these difficult times.
