Cerney News Announcement
MAY 2013

PARISH COUNCIL CHAIRMAN, VICE CHAIRMAN AND COMMITTEES

At last week’s Parish Council meeting elections took place to appoint officers and committees for the coming year. All members agreed to remain in the same roles as previously listed in the Village Directory and on the Parish Council web site www.southcerney.org.uk.

NEW PUBLIC SEATS

The Parish Council will install a new public seat, opposite the War Memorial, to replace the old wooden one which was vandalised beyond repair. This seat had been erected in memory of Jesse Price a well known figure in his time in the village. Another seat to commemorate the 60th anniversary of the coronation of Queen Elizabeth II will be placed opposite the bus stop on land adjacent to Kingfisher Place.

COMPOST SCHEME

This scheme is sponsored by Gloucestershire County Council as it saves it the cost of hauling the green waste across the county for processing. The Parish Council receives payments for every tonne produced and have the finished compost for disposal as we wish. If you can assist by bringing your green waste to the Upper Up site or want some compost please contact Mike Stuart 01285 860439.

BT TELEPHONE KIOSK

For the past few months vandals continue to break the glass panes in the telephone kiosk in High Street. The Parish Council appeals to those individuals responsible to stop this moronic behaviour because if they are caught action will be certainly taken against them.

PARISH COUNCIL ANNUAL PARISH MEETING

On Wednesday 15th May the Parish Council held its Annual Parish Meeting in the Reformed Church in Clarks Hay. About 40 people attended to hear reports from the various local representatives including the Parish Council, Cotswold District Council, Gloucestershire County Council, Ann Edwards C of E School, Revd Canon D. Bowers, Revd James Taylor and finally from the Village Hall Management Committee.

OPEN DAYS AT 29 REGIMENT, DUKE OF GLOUCESTER BARRACKS

Dave Martin announced at the Annual Parish Meeting on Wednesday 15th May that Open Days will be held on 10th and 11th July. This event is primarily aimed at schools and local dignitaries, but there may be interest from others in the parish who sometimes wonder what might be going on behind the wire. It may be possible to include a selective group from the village as long as it is coordinated through a central point. If you are interested in joining a group please contact Maurice McKee on 01285 644864 or by email clerk@southcerney.org.uk and he will liaise with Mr Martin in organising a visit.

REFURBISHED ENTRANCE TO THE UPPER UP CAR PARK

The refurbishment of the Upper Up car park continues with the completion of a new entrance which should prevent large vehicles from entering and causing surface damage. The redesign of the parking area with bays separated by wooden slats will help to stop those irresponsible drivers from creating doughnuts in the gravel surface. Plans are being discussed to provide a hard wearing surface which will make the area more user friendly especially in wet weather.

HORIZONTAL SWING AT ROBERT FRANKLIN WAY PLAY AREA

After many months of inaction, partly through the activity of vandals and wear of the links which made the contraption hazardous, the swing is once again back in working order.
