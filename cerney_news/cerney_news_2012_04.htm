Cerney News Announcement
APRIL 2012

ROBERT FRANKLIN WAY PLAY AREA

The Parish Council agreed to let Gloucestershire Highways use part of its playing field at Robert Franklin Way to erect a compound to house site facilities and to store building materials and plant for two four week periods while the two old railway bridges are repaired. The two periods are necessary to allow for bat roosting at the bridges but all the compound and buildings will be removed and the site renovated between the two events. Although there will be some loss of grass play area, there will still be a more than adequate space left for the youngsters to play football in. The Parish Council negotiated some refurbishment of the wooden fence and the larger play equipment as compensation for the use of the play area.

SEAT TO COMMEMORATE THE GOLDEN JUBILEE OF QUEEN ELIZABETH II

The Parish Council has installed a new seat with a plaque overlooking the water area to the west of the Upper Up playing fields to commemorate the Golden Jubilee of Queen Elizabeth II.

WILLOW LANTERN PAGEANT

The Cotswold Water Park Trust Willow Pageant this year was a great success. There were over 450 participants attending with some incredible willow lanterns -- Giant Foxes, Badgers, Bats and Glow Worms! The Pageant was generously supported by CDC Youth Funding, GAVAC, South Cerney Parish Council, Minety Parish Council and Malmesbury Community Board. The In Our Element Arts Team -- Lesley Greene and John Kimberley would like to say a huge thank you to the Willow Artists, Olá Samba, Amber Audio, local community groups and schools, New Brewery Arts and Londis store for selling the willow packs. On behalf of Johno -- many thanks to the team of stewards who ensured the safety of all those attending.
