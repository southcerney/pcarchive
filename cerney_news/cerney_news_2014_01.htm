Cerney News Announcement
JANUARY 2014

EMERGENCY PLAN

Parish Council has several volunteers from the community willing to help set up an Emergency Plan for the parish. Using advice from County Council, District Council and from Gloucestershire Rural Community Council, the group is preparing a draft plan designed to deal with every type of emergency, not only flooding, that could possibly affect us. The aim is then to role out the scheme involving as many of the organisations, clubs and individuals to ensure that everyone is aware of what to do should any emergency hits us.

FLOOD ALLEVIATION WORKS

Cotswold District Council, working with Glos Highways, has recently carried out essential improvements to a collapsed drain on the outskirts of the village at Upper Up. During the floods of last winter, and in previous years, water from the roadside ditch was unable to access the main drain and flooded the roadway. After initial exploratory works carried out by local volunteers who found that the drain was blocked the authorities have now renewed the pipes.

SEASON'S GREETINGS

The Chairman, Vice Chairman, Parish Councillors and Clerk wish the residents of South Cerney and Cerney Wick a very happy Christmas and best wishes for the New Year.

BE PREPARED FOR FLOODING

If you live anywhere that is likely to be flooded please ensure that you have obtained your own supply of sand bags and have taken all possible steps to protect your property and possessions. Such supplies will not be available from Parish Council.

VILLAGE PLAN

The new Village Plan has been formally adopted by Parish Council which will use it as their mandate for the next few years. To see the new Plan go to www.southcerney.org.uk. We also intend to include the details of the Plan in the next edition of the Cerney News.

CERNEY WICK PLAYGROUND

After some excellent fund raising by the people of Cerney Wick and obtaining some large grants from various sources the playground is being totally renewed. Work should be finalised by the beginning of February, depending on weather conditions, but until then the facility has to be kept closed.

R-WAY YOUTH GROUP

After completing their intensive training course in Youth Leadership, financed by CDC, Jasmin Tresadern and Chris Wearing are now assistant leaders for the R-Way Youth Group here in Cerney. They recently received their certificates from our District Councillors. The group meets on Monday evenings at the Scout building in Berkeley Close with the junior’s session 4-6pm and the senior’s 7-9pm.
