Cerney News Announcement
OCTOBER 2009


BARRACKS FOOTPATH

The Barracks footpath has finally been officially added to the definitive map of public rights of way in Gloucestershire. However, works have yet to be carried out to clear the bushes that are blocking the path and also the provision of a metal kissing gate at the school end.

DOG MESS

Complaints have been received from residents in the Beverstone Road and Berkeley Close areas about the amount of dog mess being left in public places which is not recovered by the owners of the offending animals. If your animal leaves its mess in public, please clear it up.

YOUTH PARISH COUNCIL

Jasmin Tresadem is recruiting members to join the Youth Palish Council. If you are interested in applying please contact Jasmin on 07791 321951.

RED TELEPHONE BOXES

British Telecom has offered its two red telephone boxes in the Parish for adoption at a cost of £1 each. The Parish Council will debate a resolution whether or not to accept the offer at its next meeting on November 11th.

GLOUCESTERSHIRE FIRE & RESCUE SERVICE

The service is currently visiting homes in the area offering FREE home fire safety checks. Telephone 0800 180 41 40.
