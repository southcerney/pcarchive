Cerney News Announcement
March 2008


YOUTH PARISH COUNCIL RECRUITS
The Youth Parish Council would like to encourage young persons in the age range 11-18 to join it to promote ideas and schemes which will be of benefit to them. Younger volunteers will be especially welcome. If enough show interest and wish to carry on there may have to be an election. Work on the Upper Up cycle mounds will start shortly after Easter and any serious ideas and help will be welcomed. This is your chance to have a "hands on" decision in the creation of your own play area. For further information contact joelodge@hotmail.co.uk

FLOOD FAIR
Following flooding in parts of the Cotswolds last July, and earlier this year, the Environment Agency, the National Flood Forum and Cotswold District Council have organised a Flood Protection Products Fair at the Bingham Hall, Cirencester on Friday 28th March between 2.30pm-7pm..

MOBILE LIBRARY ARRANGEMENTS
Please check out the notice board for the new times and parts of the village which the mobile library will visit.

DOG MESS
Although the majority of dog owners responsibly clean up any mess left by their pets there are still those about who antisocially ignore all requests to do so. The District Council is considering which dog control orders it will adopt under its new power that will include the issuing of Fixed Penalty Notices for offences. You have been warned..

STREET TRADING (MOBILE ICE CREAM SALES)
Two applications for licences to permit the sale of ice cream from mobile dispensers in South Cerney were rejected by the Parish Council at its last meeting.

POLICE INCIDENT COMMUNICATIONS
A survey of those attending the Parish Council meeting on how satisfied they were with the response from the operators answering 0845 090 1234 was very positive. All incidents should be reported to this number if the case is a non emergency event..
