Cerney News Announcement
FEBRUARY 2014

FLOODING

Although our parish was threatened with severe flooding over Christmas into early January, thankfully few dwellings actually had water enter their living accommodation. The flood relief works at the old railway embankment at Robert Franklin Way carried out in 2012/13 kept the level of the river Churn at the same maximum height as the previous emergency. Also the more recent renewal of fractured drain pipes at Upper Up prevented any re-occurrence of the highway being flooded in the built-up area. Unfortunately, the sewers and the pumping stations owned by Thames Water proved to be incapable of dealing with the excess water draining into them. Far too many households had sewage backing up making life very difficult. This time it seems that problems were experienced over a wider part of the village and it would be helpful if those who had problems could contact Parish Council to report what happened. This information is needed to convince the authorities that works have to be done to improve the situation. The Parish Council does not and cannot provide sand bags or other flood prevention aids. If you live in an area liable to flooding, you must make you own arrangements.

REPOSITIONING OF THE WAR MEMORIAL

The repositioning of the War Memorial as required to improve the safety of the High Street with Broadway Lane junction will take place over the next few weeks. Sometime in February the Memorial will be dismantled and removed to a stonemason's yard for cleaning and renovation while the new road junction is constructed. The Memorial will then be rebuilt in its new position approximately four metres nearer the cottage. It is planned that the works should be completed by the end of April. The improvements to the junction of Spine Road with Broadway Lane are due to commence in April/May and should take approximately four weeks to finish.

NEXT FINANCIAL YEAR’S COUNCIL TAX PRECEPT

The Parish Council has accepted the recommendation of its Finance Committee regarding the precept for the forthcoming financial year, April 2014 to March 2015. Through prudent management of resources and tight budgetary control the Parish Council has been able to set a precept which will result in a small increase in the base Band “D” demand of 86p annually. The Parish Council will support several community groups through the Village Plan scheme. Those who will benefit include Cerney Wick replacement play equipment, the South Cerney Art Group, South Cerney Youth and Adult Football clubs, South Cerney Scouts, South Cerney Street Fair and the Centenary Commemorations for World War I. Other smaller donations will be made to groups which will be decided at the March meeting of the Parish Council. The approved budget can be viewed on www.southcerney.org.uk

WORLD WAR I COMMEMORATIONS

The Parish Council appeals for any relative of those who fell during the 1914–1918 World War who may have memorabilia or photographs and willing to lend them to the Parish Council for display to contact Cllr Peter Jay on 860641.

EMERGENCY PLAN

Working with a large team of volunteers from the community, the Parish Council is setting up an Emergency Plan. Working with both CDC and Gloucestershire County Council advisors and representatives from Bromford Housing Association, it is intended that the Plan will cover most eventualities for any disaster that might affect the parish. Further details will be reported in this and other publications in due course.
