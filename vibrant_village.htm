Judges' write-up - Gloucestershire Vibrant Village of the Year 2010

South Cerney

The judges were impressed by the community spirit demonstrated in such a large village. The way in which the churches worked together was of particular note. The village's choice of Village Hall and Reform Church as their best facility seemed well founded and the way they worked in concord meant that had to be listed jointly.

The project which impressed was the creation of a new allotment from scratch. Once the negotiations to obtain the use of a site had been completed, great efforts on the part of the new allotment holders meant that the site is in almost full production after only a matter of weeks.

The needs of young people in the village are always taken into account and they have an active youth parish council in place. Young people played a part in the design of the play area which is a fine facility which the young people value.

South Cerney also shows itself as a caring community, both locally in its support for victims of violence and outside the UK with the aid it supplies to the victims of the tsunami in Sri Lanka.

 

Rosie Woolley
Philip Bennett
