Notes:
1.
Interest paid on cash investments reflects the continuing low returns on bank deposits. As funds held
by the Parish Council do not qualify under the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) it
was decided to reduce its risk to a bank failure by distributing its deposit funds to Barclays Bank,
HSBC, Lloyds Bank and Santander Bank. This policy continues.
2.
Adjusted for wayleave paid in March 2013 of £34.31.
3.
Grants received from Biffa for the refurbishment of Cerney Wick play area. Further grants will be
paid on completion of the project which has been delayed by the very wet weather experienced during
December 2013, January and February 2014.
4.
Residents or relatives of residents pay for memorial trees to be planted.
5.
South Cerney Cricket Club and South Cerney Football Club pay for their portion of the premium.
6.
Under a plan to reduce green waste collection the Parish Council operates a composting scheme the
result of which Gloucestershire County Council rewards the production by a weight measure
calculation of its output.
7.
Santander returned a bond it had to Parish Council funds.
8.
Added to the gross cost of employing the Clerk are the NIC plus HMRC tax contributions for the two
new Youth Workers who are now employees of the Parish Council.
9.
Emergency flood relief work over Christmas 2013-early 2014 period.
10.
The Section 137 payments are well within the allowed limits for such disbursements.
11.
Further to the Parish Council’s commitment to its youth and due to the withdrawal of the
Gloucestershire County Council’s youth provision, under the auspices of the Village Plan the Parish
Council continued its support of activities and the youth workers.
12.
Planning for the building of the new Community Hall, subsidised through a s.106 Agreement with
Redrow Homes South West continues. The plans are reviewed through open meetings with the
residents on a regular basis. Completion of the project is expected to be late summer 2016.
13.
The Parish Council, through its Village Plan, continues to support groups. These grants were made to
village organisations: Youth Football, Senior Football, United Church, All Hallows Parochial Church
Council, Brownies, Cotswold Water Park Trust and Neighbourhood Watch.
14.
The deficit is mostly accounted for by the expense of planning for the Community Hall. The s.106
grant in 2012-2013 has financed this deficit.
Annual Return
The Parish Council is not responsible for any trust funds.
